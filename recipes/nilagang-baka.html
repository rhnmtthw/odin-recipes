Nilagang Baka

Nilagang Baka, which translates to "boiled beef," holds a special place in Filipino cuisine as a hearty and nourishing dish that is popular across all regions of the Philippines. Its preparation involves simmering beef cuts in water until tender, imparting a rich and comforting flavor to the broth. This dish is often prepared for family gatherings and special occasions, where its aroma fills the kitchen and invites everyone to the dining table.

The choice of vegetables in Nilagang Baka varies but typically includes potatoes, cabbage, and sometimes corn on the cob, all of which absorb the savory essence of the beef broth during cooking. The simplicity of ingredients allows the natural flavors to shine through, creating a dish that is both wholesome and satisfying. Nilagang Baka not only showcases Filipino culinary tradition but also embodies the warmth and hospitality that are integral to Filipino culture.

Ingredients needed for Nilagang Baka

  • 1 lb beef (brisket or shank), cut into chunks
  • 4-5 cups water
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 1 small cabbage, quartered
  • 2 corn on the cob, halved
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps on cooking Nilagang Baka:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil
  2. Add the beef chunks and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours or until tender
  3. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface
  4. Add potatoes and corn, simmer for 10 minutes
  5. Add cabbage and cook for another 5 minutes until vegetables are tender
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice
